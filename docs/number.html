Number and Operations

In this unit we build confident number sense for Year 9 by connecting place value, rounding, and scientific notation with efficient operations and clear mathematical language.

Lesson notes and practice

Week 1 Thu - Unit launch, number line warm-up, baseline check

Notes

  • Review integer placement on a number line and compare values using <, >, and =.
  • Emphasize direction: right is greater, left is smaller.
  • Baseline check focuses on place value and simple operations.

Worked examples

  1. Place and compare: \(-3\) and \(2\).
    • \(-3\) is left of \(2\), so \(-3 < 2\).
  2. Distance from zero: compare \(-7\) and \(5\).
    • \(|-7| = 7\) and \(|5| = 5\), so \(-7\) is further from zero.
  3. Order these: \(4, -1, 0, -5\).
    • \(-5, -1, 0, 4\).

Practice questions

  1. Plot \(-6, -2, 0, 3, 7\) on a number line.
  2. Which is greater: \(-4\) or \(-9\)?
  3. Order: \(6, -3, -8, 1\) from least to greatest.
  4. Write a number between \(-2\) and \(3\).
  5. True or false: \(-1 > -3\).
  6. What is the distance between \(-4\) and \(2\)?
  7. Place value: in \(54.203\), what is the value of the 2?
  8. Evaluate: \(3 + (-5)\).

Week 1 Fri - Place value review, decimals as parts of a whole

Notes

  • Place value extends to tenths, hundredths, thousandths.
  • Decimals represent parts of a whole and can be compared by place value.

Worked examples

  1. Write \(3\) tenths and \(7\) hundredths as a decimal: \(0.37\).
  2. Compare \(0.5\) and \(0.47\): \(0.50 > 0.47\).
  3. Expand \(12.304\) by place value: \(10 + 2 + 0.3 + 0.004\).

Practice questions

  1. Write \(6\) tenths and \(2\) thousandths as a decimal.
  2. Which is larger: \(0.209\) or \(0.29\)?
  3. Expand \(4.508\) by place value.
  4. Write \(7.03\) in words.
  5. Compare using <, >, =: \(1.25\) and \(1.205\).
  6. What is the value of the 8 in \(3.081\)?
  7. Order: \(0.4, 0.04, 0.44, 0.404\).
  8. Convert \(3\) and \(5\) tenths to a decimal.

Week 2 Mon - Rounding decimals to place value

Notes

  • Identify the rounding digit and look one place to the right.
  • If the next digit is 5 or more, round up; otherwise round down.

Worked examples

  1. Round \(6.247\) to the nearest tenth: \(6.2\).
  2. Round \(12.995\) to the nearest hundredth: \(13.00\).
  3. Round \(0.0499\) to the nearest hundredth: \(0.05\).

Practice questions

  1. Round \(4.738\) to the nearest tenth.
  2. Round \(9.995\) to the nearest hundredth.
  3. Round \(15.039\) to the nearest hundredth.
  4. Round \(0.504\) to the nearest tenth.
  5. Round \(3.14159\) to the nearest thousandth.
  6. Round \(62.499\) to the nearest whole number.
  7. Round \(1.205\) to the nearest tenth.
  8. Round \(7.444\) to the nearest hundredth.

Week 2 Tue - Significant figures

Notes

  • All non-zero digits are significant.
  • Zeros between non-zero digits are significant.
  • Leading zeros are not significant.
  • Trailing zeros are significant only if a decimal point is shown.

Worked examples

  1. \(0.00450\) has 3 significant figures.
  2. \(1200\) has 2 significant figures; \(1200.\) has 4 significant figures.
  3. Round \(0.06749\) to 2 significant figures: \(0.067\).

Practice questions

  1. How many significant figures are in \(0.03040\)?
  2. How many significant figures are in \(7050\)?
  3. Round \(3.4567\) to 3 significant figures.
  4. Round \(98.76\) to 2 significant figures.
  5. Round \(0.009912\) to 2 significant figures.
  6. Write \(0.004560\) with 3 significant figures.
  7. True or false: \(100.0\) has 4 significant figures.
  8. Round \(12,450\) to 3 significant figures.

Week 2 Wed - Recurring decimals and notation

Notes

  • Recurring decimals repeat forever; use a dot or bar to show the repeat.
  • Convert fractions to decimals by division.
  • Recognize common recurring forms like \(\frac{1}{3} = 0.\overline{3}\).

Worked examples

  1. \(\frac{1}{3} = 0.\overline{3}\).
  2. \(\frac{2}{11} = 0.\overline{18}\).
  3. \(0.\overline{6} = \frac{2}{3}\).

Practice questions

  1. Convert \(\frac{1}{6}\) to a decimal (recurring).
  2. Convert \(\frac{5}{9}\) to a decimal.
  3. Write \(0.\overline{72}\) as a fraction in simplest form.
  4. Which is larger: \(0.\overline{4}\) or \(0.\overline{5}\)?
  5. Convert \(0.\overline{3}\) to a fraction.
  6. Convert \(\frac{7}{12}\) to a decimal (recurring or terminating).
  7. Identify the repeating block in \(0.142857142857...\).
  8. True or false: every fraction gives a terminating decimal.

Week 2 Thu - Scientific notation and powers of 10

Notes

  • Scientific notation is \(a \times 10^n\) where \(1 \le a < 10\).
  • Moving the decimal left makes \(n\) positive; right makes \(n\) negative.

Worked examples

  1. \(3,500,000 = 3.5 \times 10^6\).
  2. \(0.00072 = 7.2 \times 10^{-4}\).
  3. \(4.2 \times 10^3 = 4200\).

Practice questions

  1. Write \(56,000\) in scientific notation.
  2. Write \(0.0045\) in scientific notation.
  3. Convert \(9.1 \times 10^2\) to standard form.
  4. Convert \(3.07 \times 10^{-3}\) to standard form.
  5. Which is larger: \(6.2 \times 10^5\) or \(8.1 \times 10^4\)?
  6. Write \(12,300,000\) in scientific notation.
  7. Write \(0.0000005\) in scientific notation.
  8. Estimate \(4.9 \times 10^3 + 3.1 \times 10^3\).

Week 2 Fri - No school

Notes

  • No lesson (public holiday).

Optional practice

  1. Review rounding to the nearest tenth.
  2. Convert \(0.\overline{3}\) to a fraction.
  3. Write \(45,000\) in scientific notation.
  4. Round \(0.0999\) to 2 significant figures.

Week 3 Mon - Mixed practice: rounding and significant figures

Notes

  • Choose rounding to place value or significant figures based on the question.
  • Check reasonableness after rounding.

Worked examples

  1. Round \(0.006789\) to 2 significant figures: \(0.0068\).
  2. Round \(45.376\) to the nearest tenth: \(45.4\).
  3. Round \(98,650\) to 3 significant figures: \(98,700\).

Practice questions

  1. Round \(2.149\) to the nearest hundredth.
  2. Round \(0.0009401\) to 2 significant figures.
  3. Round \(65.05\) to 3 significant figures.
  4. Round \(4.995\) to the nearest hundredth.
  5. Round \(3.0049\) to 2 significant figures.
  6. Round \(12,340\) to 2 significant figures.
  7. Round \(0.05199\) to the nearest thousandth.
  8. Round \(8.888\) to 2 significant figures.

Week 3 Tue - Scientific notation and calculator checks

Notes

  • Use calculator EXP or EE button for powers of 10.
  • Check if answers are reasonable by comparing magnitude.

Worked examples

  1. \(6.5 \times 10^4 = 65,000\).
  2. \(0.0000092 = 9.2 \times 10^{-6}\).
  3. \(3.1 \times 10^5 + 2.4 \times 10^5 = 5.5 \times 10^5\).

Practice questions

  1. Convert \(8.03 \times 10^7\) to standard form.
  2. Write \(0.000120\) in scientific notation.
  3. Calculate: \(2.5 \times 10^3 + 4.8 \times 10^3\).
  4. Calculate: \(7.2 \times 10^2 - 3.5 \times 10^2\).
  5. Which is larger: \(1.2 \times 10^{-3}\) or \(9.5 \times 10^{-4}\)?
  6. Convert \(4,600,000\) to scientific notation.
  7. Convert \(6.7 \times 10^{-2}\) to standard form.
  8. Estimate \(9.9 \times 10^6 - 1.1 \times 10^6\).

Week 3 Wed - Words for +, -, x, /

Notes

  • Identify operation words: add, sum, total; subtract, difference; multiply, product; divide, quotient.
  • Translate word problems into number sentences.

Worked examples

  1. “The sum of 7 and 9” is \(7 + 9\).
  2. “The difference between 15 and 6” is \(15 - 6\).
  3. “The quotient of 48 and 6” is \(48 \div 6\).

Practice questions

  1. Write a number sentence for: “The product of 8 and 5”.
  2. Write a number sentence for: “The sum of 3, 7, and 12”.
  3. Write a number sentence for: “12 less than 40”.
  4. Write a number sentence for: “The quotient of 54 and 9”.
  5. Write a number sentence for: “The difference between 100 and 35”.
  6. Solve: “A packet has 24 stickers shared equally among 6 students”.
  7. Solve: “A total of 45 apples are packed into 5 boxes equally”.
  8. Solve: “A $12 discount is taken from a $65 item”.

Week 3 Thu - Integers

Notes

  • Use the number line to model integer addition and subtraction.
  • Adding a negative moves left; subtracting a negative moves right.

Worked examples

  1. \(-4 + 7 = 3\).
  2. \(-6 - 5 = -11\).
  3. \(8 + (-12) = -4\).

Practice questions

  1. \(-3 + 9\)
  2. \(5 + (-8)\)
  3. \(-12 + 7\)
  4. \(-6 - 4\)
  5. \(9 - (-2)\)
  6. \(-15 - (-5)\)
  7. \(-7 + (-6)\)
  8. \(14 + (-20)\)

Week 4 Mon - Order of operations

Notes

  • Use BEDMAS: Brackets, Exponents, Division/Multiplication, Addition/Subtraction.
  • Division and multiplication are done left to right, same for addition and subtraction.

Worked examples

  1. \(3 + 4 \times 2 = 3 + 8 = 11\).
  2. \((5 + 7) \div 3 = 12 \div 3 = 4\).
  3. \(2^3 + 6 \times 2 = 8 + 12 = 20\).

Practice questions

  1. \(8 + 2 \times 5\)
  2. \((18 - 6) \div 3\)
  3. \(4^2 + 3 \times 4\)
  4. \(20 - 6 \div 2\)
  5. \((7 + 3) \times 4\)
  6. \(5 + 12 \div 3\)
  7. \(30 \div 5 + 6\)
  8. \(2(3 + 5) - 4\)

Introduction

Number and operations form the foundation of mathematics. In this unit, we’ll develop fluency with different types of numbers and operations.

Key Topics

Integers

Working with positive and negative numbers:

  • Understanding the number line
  • Adding and subtracting integers
  • Multiplying and dividing integers
  • Real-world applications of negative numbers

Example: If the temperature is -3°C and it rises by 7°C, what is the new temperature?

Solution: -3 + 7 = 4°C

Fractions

Operations with fractions:

  • Equivalent fractions
  • Adding and subtracting fractions
  • Multiplying and dividing fractions
  • Mixed numbers and improper fractions

Example: Calculate \(\frac{2}{3} + \frac{1}{4}\)

Solution: \(\frac{8}{12} + \frac{3}{12} = \frac{11}{12}\)

Decimals

Understanding and using decimal numbers:

  • Place value
  • Adding and subtracting decimals
  • Multiplying and dividing decimals
  • Converting between fractions and decimals

Example: 3.5 × 0.4 = 1.4

Percentages

Working with percentages:

  • Understanding percentages as fractions of 100
  • Converting between percentages, decimals, and fractions
  • Finding percentages of amounts
  • Percentage increase and decrease

Example: What is 15% of 80?

Solution: 15% × 80 = 0.15 × 80 = 12

Practice Problems

  1. Calculate: (-5) + 8 - (-3)
  2. Simplify: \(\frac{3}{4} \times \frac{2}{5}\)
  3. Find 25% of 160
  4. Convert 0.625 to a fraction in simplest form
