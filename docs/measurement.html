Measurement

Introduction

Measurement involves quantifying attributes of objects and events. We’ll learn about different units and how to measure length, area, volume, and other quantities.

Key Topics

Units of Measurement

Understanding metric units:

  • Length: millimetres (mm), centimetres (cm), metres (m), kilometres (km)
  • Mass: grams (g), kilograms (kg), tonnes (t)
  • Capacity: millilitres (mL), litres (L)
  • Time: seconds, minutes, hours, days

Example: Convert 2.5 km to metres.

Solution: 2.5 km = 2.5 × 1000 = 2500 m

Perimeter

Measuring the distance around shapes:

  • Perimeter of rectangles and squares
  • Perimeter of triangles and other polygons
  • Perimeter of composite shapes

Example: Find the perimeter of a rectangle with length 8 cm and width 5 cm.

Solution: Perimeter = 2(8 + 5) = 2(13) = 26 cm

Area

Measuring the space inside shapes:

  • Area of rectangles and squares: \(A = l \times w\)
  • Area of triangles: \(A = \frac{1}{2} \times b \times h\)
  • Area of circles: \(A = \pi r^2\)
  • Area of composite shapes

Example: Find the area of a triangle with base 10 cm and height 6 cm.

Solution: \(A = \frac{1}{2} \times 10 \times 6 = 30 \text{ cm}^2\)

Volume

Measuring the space inside 3D objects:

  • Volume of rectangular prisms: \(V = l \times w \times h\)
  • Volume of cubes: \(V = s^3\)
  • Volume of cylinders: \(V = \pi r^2 h\)
  • Capacity and volume relationship

Example: Find the volume of a cube with side length 4 cm.

Solution: \(V = 4^3 = 64 \text{ cm}^3\)

Surface Area

Measuring the total area of all faces:

  • Surface area of rectangular prisms
  • Surface area of cubes
  • Using nets to find surface area

Example: Find the surface area of a cube with side length 3 cm.

Solution: A cube has 6 faces, each with area \(3^2 = 9\) cm²

Total surface area = \(6 \times 9 = 54\) cm²

Scale and Maps

Working with scaled representations:

  • Understanding scale ratios
  • Using scales to find actual distances
  • Map reading

Example: On a map with scale 1:50000, a distance is 4 cm. What is the actual distance?

Solution: Actual distance = 4 × 50000 = 200000 cm = 2 km

Practice Problems

  1. Convert 3500 mL to litres
  2. Find the perimeter of a square with side length 7 cm
  3. Calculate the area of a circle with radius 5 cm (use π ≈ 3.14)
  4. Find the volume of a rectangular prism with dimensions 6 cm × 4 cm × 3 cm
